"Hello Sharing World"

CREATIVE COMMONS GLOBAL SUMMIT 2015

A fun festival where people from more than 80 countries who believe in the value of sharing and openness and create positive change gather and share ideas will be held in Korea in October.

2015.10.14 (Wed) ~ 17 (Sat)

@ National Museum of Korea, Art Center Nabi, etc.

Main Program

  • Lecture

  • Listen to lectures from speakers from around the world and learn about the latest trends in the world.

  • CC Exhibition

  • Let's meet the creations of artists from various fields who share the open spirit of CC.

  • Forum

  • We have time to share our thoughts and opinions with experts by topic.

  • Workshop

  • You can create your own creations using open source instead of just looking at them.

  • CC Ignite

  • Create opportunities to freely network with CC personnel from around the world.

Featured Speakers

  • Yochai Benkler

  • Professor at Harvard Law School

  • Focusing on a collaborative approach in a networked environment, he created the concept of co-production based on sharing, such as open source software and Wikipedia.

  • SohYeong Noh

  • Art Center Nabi Director, CC Korea Director

  • As the author of , he opened 'Art Center Nabi', Korea's first digital art institution, in 2000, and is currently serving.

  • Layla Tretikov

  • Secretary General of the Wikimedia Foundation

  • Layla Tretikov is the general secretary of the Wikimedia Foundation, a non-profit organization that runs Wikipedia. Wikipedia is provided free of charge in 290 languages ​​every month to over 100 million people, nearly half of the world's population.

  • Jeon Gil-nam

  • Emeritus Professor, Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST)

  • By developing Asia's first Internet protocol network SDN and leading Korea's first private line Internet connection in 1990, it ushered in the era of the Internet in earnest.

  • Julia Leda

  • Head of the Young Pirates of Europe

  • European integration and online youth participation in politics and democracy are major concerns, and a report has been published that will potentially affect the revision of the EU's copyright law in July.

  • Ryan Merkley

  • Creative Commons CEO, Former Mozilla Foundation COO

  • He led open source projects at the Mozilla Foundation and joined CC as CEO in 2014. He has been active in open movements such as open government and open source.
